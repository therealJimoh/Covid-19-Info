CORONAVIRUS (COVID-19)

Coronavirus also know as Covid-19 is a highly contagious infectious disease caused by a newly identified virus called severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2), which belongs to the coronavirus family (Kathleen, 2020). The first cases were seen in Wuhan, China, in December 2019 before spreading globally

Let's bust some myths circulating. SARS-CoV is zoonotic in origin - virus that circulate among animals with some also known to infect humans. The virus is transmitted through direct contact with respiratory droplets of an infected person (generated through coughing and sneezing), and touching surfaces contaminated with the virus. The COVID-19 virus may survive on surfaces for several hours. These droplets can then be inhaled, or they can land on surfaces that others may come into contact with, who can then get infected when they touch their nose, mouth or eyes. The virus can survive on different surfaces from several hours (copper, cardboard) up to a few days (plastic and stainless steel) but simple disinfectants can kill it (ECDPC, 2020).

On 10 April 2020, the number of cases of confirmed COVID-19 globally was over 1.56 million 5. COVID-19 has now been diagnosed in 184 territories, in six continents according to an online virus tracker created by the medical journal, The Lancet, and hosted by Johns Hopkins University.

SYMPTOMS

COVID-19 typically presents with systemic and/or respiratory manifestations. Some individuals infected with SARS-CoV-2 are asymptomatic and can act as carriers. The symptoms are usually mild and tend to begin 2–14 days after exposure. Although the symptoms may vary from person to person, the CDC reports the most common symptoms as:

  • a fever
  • a cough, which is usually dry
  • shortness of breath
  • sore throat

WHO states that other symptoms can include:

  • aches and pains
  • in rare cases, diarrhoe, nausea, or a runny nose
  • palpitation, chest tightness
The virus is still being studies and full list of syptoms are still being investigated.

PREVENTIVE MEASURES

There is currently no cure/vaccine for covid-19 but you can protect yourself from contracting it and help prevent the spread of the virus with everyday preventive actions:

  • STAY HOME!!
  • Avoid close contact(1-3 meter feet) with people who are sick
  • Avoid touching your eyes, nose, and mouth with unwashed hands
  • Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds. Use an alcohol-based hand sanitizer that contains at least 60% alcohol if soap and water are not available.
  • Cover your nose and mouth with a disposable tissue or flexed elbow when you cough or sneeze
  • Stay home and self-isolate from others in the household if you feel unwell
  • If you travelled from an affected area, you should self-isolate yourself for 14days and according to the NCDC guildlines.
  • Clean and disinfect frequently touched objects and surfaces

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQ)

I know you have questions to ask and I hope this answers all the questions bothering you.

How Does the COVID-19 Virus Spread?

The virus is transmitted through direct contact with respiratory droplets of an infected person (generated through coughing and sneezing), and touching surfaces contaminated with the virus. The COVID-19 virus may survive on surfaces for several hours, but simple disinfectants can kill it.

What Causes Coronaviruses?

Coronaviruses are zoonotic. This means they first develop in animals before developing in humans. For the virus to pass from animal to humans, a person has to come into close contact with an animal that carries the infection.Once the virus develops in people, coronaviruses can be spread from person to person through respiratory droplets.

Is there a Vaccine?

There is currently no vaccine to protect against COVID-19. The best way to prevent infection is to take everyday preventive actions, like avoiding close contact with people who are sick and washing your hands often.

Is there a Treatment?

There is no specific antiviral treatment for COVID-19. People with COVID-19 can seek medical care to help relieve symptoms.

Should I Wear Medical Mask?

The use of a medical mask is advised if you have respiratory symptoms (coughing or sneezing) to protect others. If you don’t have any symptoms, then there is no need to wear a mask. If masks are worn, they must be used and disposed of properly to ensure their effectiveness and to avoid any increased risk of transmitting the virus. The use of a mask alone is not enough to stop infections and must be combined with frequent hand washing, covering sneezes and coughs, and avoiding close contact with anyone with cold or flu-like symptoms (coughing, sneezing, fever).

What Should I do if a Family Member/ Friends Displays Symptoms?

You should seek medical care early if you or your close relation has a fever, cough or difficulty breathing. Consider calling ahead to tell your health care provider. If you have traveled to an area where COVID-19 has been reported, or if you have been in close contact with someone with who has traveled from one of these areas and has respiratory symptoms. You should pick up your phone and dial the NCDC covid-19 hotlines.

Is There Specific Drug for Covid-19?

NOT YET! However clinical trials are on going to find an effective drug.

Who Can Get Tested?

Anyone with travel history outside Nigeria, who presents with fever, cough or breathing with fever, cough or breathing difficulties within 14 days of arrival and anyone who has demonstrated the symptoms.

How Do I Stop the Spread of Covid-19?

It is your responsibility to STAY IN YOUR HOUSE and avoid mass gathering of people. Practice good public hygiene.
